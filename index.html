What Depletes You. What Restores You.

Your energy assessment gives us a snapshot. Now let's get specific...because knowing where you're low is only half the picture. The other half is understanding what's draining the tank and what fills it back up.

Reflection: Physical Energy

Think about your physical energy right now...your sleep, your body, your movement. What is depleting your physical energy most during this job search? What, even one small thing, restores it?

Consider: Are you sleeping well or lying awake with racing thoughts? Are you skipping meals? Moving your body, even a little? Physical depletion during job search is almost universal...disrupted routines, stress hormones, sitting for long stretches. Even noticing this is useful data.

Reflection: Emotional Energy

Which relationships or situations drain your emotional energy most right now? And where...even briefly...do you feel something like relief or connection?

Think about specific people...are there conversations that leave you feeling worse? Also notice what, if anything, feels lighter. A conversation with a friend? A moment of genuine laughter? Even small islands of restoration count.

Reflection: Mental Energy

What is draining your mental clarity most right now...the decisions, the uncertainty, the constant planning? And what, even briefly, gives your mind a rest?

Job search is relentless cognitive work...tailoring applications, researching companies, rehearsing answers, managing rejection. Notice where your mental load is heaviest. Also notice what gives your brain a break...even something as simple as a walk, a podcast, or a creative task that absorbs you.

Reflection: Spiritual Energy

Job loss often attacks our sense of purpose...who we are, why what we do matters. What is most threatening your sense of meaning right now? Is there anything still connected to what matters to you?

This isn't about religion. Spiritual energy is about purpose...the sense that what you do matters, that you are part of something larger than a job title. For many people, job loss severs that connection painfully. What values, people, or work still feel meaningful to you, even if they're smaller or quieter right now?

Boundaries Are Energy Infrastructure

You've already learned that resentment is a boundary signal, and that holding a boundary under pressure is one of the hardest things we do. Now let's look at the four types of boundaries that protect the four types of energy we just mapped.

💡 The Four Boundary Types

Boundaries aren't walls. They're decisions about how you spend your finite energy. When you don't have boundaries, you leak energy...constantly, invisibly...to other people's urgency, expectations, and emotions.

⏱ Time Boundaries

How you protect your time and schedule. When you're available, when you're not. How much time you give to the search, to others, to yourself.

📱 Communication Boundaries

Who you respond to, how quickly, and in what way. This includes job search follow-ups, social media, texts from people who drain you, and unsolicited advice.

💛 Emotional Boundaries

How much of other people's emotional states you absorb. Whose anxiety, disappointment, or opinions you take on as your own.

💰 Financial Boundaries

What you will and won't spend money on, what you will and won't accept in terms of compensation or opportunities...and how you communicate these clearly.

Your Body Already Knows

We make most of our decisions from the neck up...analyzing, weighing pros and cons, second-guessing. But your body has been tracking the truth the whole time. Before a bad job takes years off your life, your gut knows. Before a promising lead turns out to be the right one, something in you lights up.

💡 Body-Based Decision Making

Somatic signals...sensations in the body...are part of your nervous system's intelligence. The same system that sends a stress response when danger is near also sends quiet signals about alignment, safety, and fit.

This practice helps you distinguish a fear signal (avoid this threat) from a values signal (this doesn't fit who I am)...and learn what a genuine YES feels like in your body.

The Job Search as Energy Management

Here's something no one tells you about looking for work: the job search itself is an energy-intensive project. And most people approach it like they have to spend everything they have, every day, to prove they deserve to find something.

This Is the Trap

Sending 40 applications from a place of desperation produces different results than sending 5 from a place of clarity. Doing a networking call when you're in shutdown produces a different impression than doing one when you've rested and regulated.

Quality of energy > quantity of effort. Always.

Your One Boundary Experiment

Research on behavior change tells us that one small, specific, practiced commitment outperforms sweeping resolutions every time. So instead of overhauling everything, we're going to pick one boundary to experiment with this week.

💡 Why "Experiment"?

Calling it an experiment takes the pressure off. You're not making a permanent declaration. You're trying something for one week to see what happens to your energy. That's all.

Choose one boundary type to focus on this week:

⏱ Time Boundary

Something about when or how long you're available

📱 Communication Boundary

Something about who or how you respond

💛 Emotional Boundary

Something about whose feelings you will and won't absorb

💰 Financial Boundary

Something about what you will and won't agree to financially

Describe Your Boundary Experiment

Be specific. What exactly will you do or not do? When? With whom? What will you say if it comes up?

The more specific, the better. Vague commitments fall apart under pressure. "I'll be better with time" is not a boundary. "I will not answer career-related calls on Sunday" is. Think about: What specifically will you stop, start, or limit? What will you say if someone pushes back? What signal will tell you the boundary is working?

Energy Is Information

Here's the shift I want to leave you with. Most of us were taught that energy is something you push through, override, or willpower your way past. The job search makes that worse...there's a cultural story that says the more depleted you are, the harder you're trying.

Energy Is Not a Weakness Signal

When you're running low, your body isn't failing you. It's giving you data. It's telling you: this is unsustainable. Something needs to change. A boundary has been crossed, or one needs to be built.

People who find work that is genuinely right for them...and build careers that last...learn to listen to that data rather than override it.

This is not soft advice. This is strategy. Regulated people make better decisions, build better relationships, and interview better. Your energy management is your competitive advantage.
